About Florian

Florian Kroker McCormack is a high-school student with a deep interest in meteorology, weather forecasting, and atmospheric science. Rather than using consumer weather apps that reduce the atmosphere to a single temperature number, Florian built his own personal weather dashboard at floriankm.com — a full forecaster-workflow workstation using live public data from NOAA, NWS, SPC, and more.

Florian's approach to weather mirrors that of a professional meteorologist: start with satellite imagery, move to radar, check surface observations and mesoanalysis, then work through short-range models and ensemble guidance. This Florian weather dashboard is the product of that disciplined curiosity.

Meteorology interests

  • Severe convection — supercell mode, tornado probability, mesoscale convective boundaries, hodograph analysis
  • Synoptic dynamics — 500 mb pattern recognition, jet-streak dynamics, extratropical cyclogenesis
  • Winter storms — precipitation type diagnosis, dendritic growth zone quality, snow ratio, banded precipitation
  • Satellite interpretation — water vapor channels, air-mass RGB, cold-top signatures, overshooting tops
  • Space weather — geomagnetic storms, Kp index, aurora forecasting, solar wind monitoring

The Florian weather dashboard

The dashboard at floriankm.com is a single-page meteorology workstation with five main sections:

  • Main dashboard — NWS point forecast, local diagnostics, radar, satellite, SPC mesoanalysis, model wall, severe weather desk, and marine/tropical tools
  • Satellite loop — Animated GOES-East loops across 8 sectors and 10 channels including GeoColor, clean IR, water vapor, and air-mass RGB
  • Space weather — Kp index, DSCOVR solar wind, aurora forecast (Ovation model), GOES X-ray flux, and SDO solar imagery
  • Marine — NDBC buoy observations, global wave height guidance, and sea surface temperature

All data is sourced from public NOAA/NWS APIs with no backend or API keys required.
